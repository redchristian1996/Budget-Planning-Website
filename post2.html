Financial Stress: Definition

Unexpected but inevitable financial problems can be difficult to overcome

Posted by Christian A. Balidoy on February 22, 2017

Anyone can suffer from financial problems are one time or another, especially during a recession. They may have lost a job or simply do not make enough to pay their bills. The Basic definition of financial problems is the difficulty in paying of debts.

Financial stress may be defined as the inability to meet one's financial obligations.

Financial stress is a sadly widespread experience. According to a poll, roughly 7 in 10 respondents are "very stressed" about money, and only 1 in 10 report that they are not stressed about finances - and the proportion of people stressed about money is only going up. This is pretty significant because financial stress is linked to health problems like depression and sleep problems. With the rising cost of living, many Americans are feeling the crunch of financial stress.

Unhealthy Coping behaviors

People experiencing financial stress can be more likely to numb their anxiety by drinking, smoking, overeating and practicing other unhealthy coping behaviors. This in turn leads to more stress.

Less Money For Self-Care

With less money in the budget, people who are already under financial stress tend to cut corners in areas like health care to pay for basic necessities like food. Small problems can go unchecked and turn into larger problems. This also leads to more stress.

To go places and do things that have never been done before – that’s what living is all about.

Lost Sleep

When under financial stress, people often experience trouble sleeping, which can add up to a sleep deficit, impairing immune functioning and cognitive abilities, causing additional moodiness, and more.

Unhealthy Emotions

Credic card debt can cause unhealthy emotions that can take a toll on health. People can experience anxiety, frustration and a sense of hopelessness as the dept piles up and increasing amounts of money are needed just to pay the interest.

This causes additional stress, which compounds with the stress from poor coping and self-neglect, to become a menacing amount of stress.
